How BlackVault Fits Into Your Workflow

1

Prepare Your USB

Download the BlackVault ISO image and flash it to a USB stick using your preferred tool. Minimum 8GB recommended, though 16GB+ is ideal for persistent storage.

2

Boot Into BlackVault

Restart your computer and select the USB as your boot device. BlackVault loads entirely into RAM without touching your hard drive.

3

Work in a Clean Room

Use the fully-featured OS for browsing, communication, document work, and more. When you shut down, all traces disappear instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does BlackVault leave any data on the host machine? +
No. BlackVault runs entirely from RAM and USB. It does not write to your hard drive or leave forensic traces. When you shut down, all session data is wiped from memory.
Can I use BlackVault on any computer? +
Yes, as long as the computer supports USB booting and meets minimum hardware requirements. BlackVault is compatible with most modern PCs and laptops.
Is BlackVault legal to use? +
Yes. Using privacy tools is legal in most jurisdictions. However, you are responsible for using BlackVault in compliance with your local laws. It is designed for legitimate privacy needs, not illegal activity.
What happens if I lose my encrypted vault password? +
Unfortunately, encrypted vaults cannot be recovered without the password. This is by design - strong encryption means no backdoors. Always keep secure backups of important passwords.
Do you provide updates and patches? +
Yes. All support plans include security updates and patches. Updates can be applied while running BlackVault if you have internet access, or by downloading new ISO releases.
Is BlackVault suitable for beginners? +
BlackVault is designed to be accessible, but it's optimized for users who understand the importance of digital privacy. We provide comprehensive documentation, and the Professional plan includes setup assistance.
